This method is particularly useful when dealing with firms whose earnings are highly volatile due to economic cycles or fluctuations in commodity prices [book:chunk 0647].

Damodaran explains that for cyclical firms, where negative earnings are often due to broader economic downturns or changes in commodity prices, the normalized earnings approach helps smooth out these fluctuations. This normalization process involves adjusting current earnings by removing temporary expenses related to the firm's specific situation and focusing on underlying trends [book:chunk 0647].

The spreadsheet tool mentioned as "normearn.xls" is designed specifically for normalizing a firm’s earnings using various methodologies, which can be applied to commodity cyclical firms like VALE. This normalization aims at providing a clearer picture of the firm's true earning potential rather than being influenced by short-term economic conditions [book:chunk 0660].

Damodaran emphasizes that when applying this approach, it is crucial to identify whether negative or low earnings are temporary issues or long-term problems. If they are temporary, replacing current earnings with normalized figures can provide a more accurate valuation of the firm's intrinsic value [book:chunk 0989]. However, if negative earnings reflect deeper structural issues within the company, this approach might overvalue the firm since it assumes quick recovery from these issues.

In summary, Damodaran recommends using normalized earnings to value commodity cyclical firms like VALE when their current earnings are distorted by short-term economic or market conditions.
