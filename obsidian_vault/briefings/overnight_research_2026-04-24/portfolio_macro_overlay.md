### Stock-Picking Value Investing
Value investing, rooted in the teachings of Benjamin Graham and refined by academics like Aswath Damodaran, emphasizes identifying undervalued stocks based on fundamental analysis. The process typically involves:

1. **Intrinsic Valuation**: Using discounted cash flow (DCF) models to estimate a company's intrinsic value.
2. **Multiples Analysis**: Applying price-to-earnings (P/E), price-to-book (P/B), and other valuation multiples to assess relative value.

The goal is to construct a portfolio of stocks that are trading below their intrinsic values, with the expectation that market prices will eventually converge towards these valuations [book:chunk 0014].

### Macro Overlay
Ray Dalio's macroeconomic overlay suggests considering broader economic conditions and cycles when making investment decisions. This involves:

- **Economic Cycles**: Understanding where the economy is in its cycle (expansion, peak, recession, trough) to anticipate market trends.
- **Monetary Policy**: Analyzing central bank actions and their impact on interest rates and liquidity.

By integrating macroeconomic insights with stock-specific valuations, investors can better time their entry into undervalued stocks or avoid overvalued ones during unfavorable economic conditions [book:chunk 0592].
