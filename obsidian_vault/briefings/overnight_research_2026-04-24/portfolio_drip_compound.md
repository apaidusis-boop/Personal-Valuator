For a long-term DRIP investor interested in both Brazilian and U.S. markets, the methods by Dalio and Damodaran that are most relevant for stock picking include valuation techniques focused on fundamental analysis and discounted cash flow (DCF) models [book:chunk 0014]. These approaches emphasize understanding a firm's financial characteristics such as growth prospects, risk profile, and cash flows to determine if stocks are under- or overvalued. The use of multiples like price-to-book value (PBV) ratios and price-to-earnings (PE) ratios is also important [book:chunk 1034]. These valuation metrics help in comparing firms across different sectors and times, providing insights into relative valuations.

Dalio's philosophy often involves identifying macroeconomic trends that can influence market conditions. For a long-term investor, understanding these broader economic factors is crucial to making informed decisions about when to enter or exit positions [book:chunk 0888]. This approach integrates technical analysis with fundamental analysis by considering how macroeconomic shifts might affect the stability of firm-specific financial metrics over time.

Damodaran's work on valuation includes detailed discussions on DCF models and relative valuation methods, which are essential tools for stock picking. These methodologies help in assessing intrinsic value based on expected future cash flows discounted at an appropriate rate [book:chunk 0112]. Additionally, Damodaran emphasizes the importance of considering market conditions and economic cycles when evaluating stocks, aligning with Dalio's emphasis on macroeconomic analysis.

In summary, for a long-term DRIP investor in BR+US markets, focusing on fundamental valuation techniques, DCF models, PBV ratios, and PE ratios as recommended by Damodaran, while integrating broader macroeconomic insights from Dalio, provides a robust framework for stock selection.
