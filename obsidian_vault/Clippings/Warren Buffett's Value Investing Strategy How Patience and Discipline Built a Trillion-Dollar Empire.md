## Invest in What You Understand

Buffett famously [avoided tech stocks](https://www.investopedia.com/berkshire-hathaway-does-not-hold-nvidia-stock-will-warren-buffett-come-to-regret-it-8600072) during the dot-com boom, explaining he didn't understand their business models. Critics called him outdated. Then the bubble burst, wiping out trillions while Berkshire kept growing. Instead of chasing the latest trends, Buffett, who is [still making investment decisions](https://www.investopedia.com/warren-buffett-says-he-is-still-making-investment-calls-at-berkshire-and-just-made-a-mystery-buy-11938021) at Berkshire, sticks to businesses with predictable cash flows and competitive advantages. ## Think Long-Term

Buffett’s investing style is often described as boring because it involves very little trading. He once said that "our favorite holding period is forever."6

Buffett buys companies with the intention of holding them through thick and thin, as long as the business fundamentals stay intact. This allows as a strong company’s earnings and dividends can grow exponentially over decades.

Buffett’s shows that you don’t need complex formulas or rapid trades to build wealth.
