## Microsoft and 11 More Stocks That Were Unfairly Punished After Earnings

Key Points

About This Summary

- 80% of Russell 1000 companies beat Q1 earnings estimates by 20% on average, with 27% earnings growth, yet stocks gained only 0.1%.
- Many companies, including Netflix and Microsoft, saw stock drops of up to 10% despite beating earnings and having estimates rise.
- A dozen stocks, excluding ServiceNow, dropped an average of 5% after beating earnings by 23%, highlighting a market disconnect.

Over the long-term, the stock market is very good at valuing companies. Over the short run, not so much. And the reaction to earnings for companies including like [Microsoft](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/msft?mod=article_chiclet)[\-0.88%](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/msft?mod=article_chiclet)

and [Netflix](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/nflx?mod=article_chiclet)[

\-3.31%

](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/nflx?mod=article_chiclet)show it.

Not every stock price reaction to recent earnings reports seems fair. Some companies have been punished for [pristine results](https://www.barrons.com/articles/te-connectivity-earnings-stock-price-8c8a875c?mod=article_inline). That can be an opportunity for investors.

Through midday trading on Monday, more than 500 companies in the [Russel 1000](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/indexes/rui?mod=article_chiclet) had reported quarterly results. Things are going well, with 80% of companies beating Wall Street estimates by an average of 20%. Earnings growth has been an impressive 27%.

That performance has landed with a thud. The average stock move after earnings is a whopping 0.1% gain. Last quarter, Russell stocks gained an average of 0.5% for 10% earnings growth.

The reason for the lackluster performance isn’t hard to suss out. Investors are nervous about high oil prices, [geopolitical tensions](https://www.barrons.com/livecoverage/stock-market-today-050426?mod=article_inline), pesky inflation, interest rates, and just about everything else.

Still, not every stock price reaction feels justified. Seaport analyst [Patrick Palfrey](https://www.barrons.com/articles/deere-stock-price-industrials-buy-now-e0f13ba5?mod=article_inline) tracks stock price moves that are “out of sync” with underlying fundamentals. For a stock to have been unjustifiably punished, it must have seen improving earnings estimates over the past three months and a lagging stock price.

For starters, there is Netflix. Its stock dropped almost 10% after its [first-quarter earnings](https://www.barrons.com/articles/netflix-earnings-stock-price-81fe1ef2?mod=article_inline), but Wall Street’s full-year 2026 earnings estimates have risen to $3.57 from $3.31 since then. Investors might be worried about second-quarter revenue and the departure of founder and chairman Reed Hastings, but the overall outlook marched higher.

That setup repeated itself in several areas. In leisure and travel, [Expedia](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/expe?mod=article_chiclet) and [Las Vegas Sands](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/lvs?mod=article_chiclet) both declined after big earnings beats, which generated higher full-year earnings estimates from the Street.

In [financials](https://www.barrons.com/livecoverage/jpmorgan-chase-bofa-citigroup-wells-fargo-bank-earnings/card/jpmorgan-traders-notch-record-revenue-during-volatile-quarter-Zh5LGJTQYuJQtWlwp5qr?mod=article_inline), the same things happened to [JPMorgan Chase](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/jpm?mod=article_chiclet)[+0.64%](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/jpm?mod=article_chiclet)

& Co., [American Express](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/axp?mod=article_chiclet), and [Mastercard](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/ma?mod=article_chiclet).

Many solid aerospace and defense earnings landed with a thud, with investors worried about the sector outlook amid rising energy prices. But 2026 earnings estimates for [GE Aerospace](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/ge?mod=article_chiclet)[+1.76%](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/ge?mod=article_chiclet)

and [RTX](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/rtx?mod=article_chiclet) rose after they reported [first-quarter numbers](https://www.barrons.com/articles/ge-aerospace-earnings-stock-price-f1336e48?mod=article_inline).

Aerospace investors can be glad they weren’t in software stocks. The sector continues to be hammered by fears that AI tools will take market share of the enterprise software market. [ServiceNow](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/now?mod=article_chiclet) was the poster child for the problem. Shares dropped almost 18% after its quarterly [earnings report](https://www.barrons.com/articles/servicenow-earnings-stock-price-4b5f8eee?mod=article_inline). That might be considered unfairly punished since full-year 2026 earnings estimates are down only four cents, or about 1%, since earnings.

Among the Magnificent Seven, Microsoft and [Meta Platforms](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/meta?mod=article_chiclet)[\-1.24%](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/meta?mod=article_chiclet)

were unfairly punished. Those two dropped [almost 4%](https://www.barrons.com/articles/microsoft-earnings-stock-price-1750a2b4?mod=article_inline) and 9% after reporting quarterly results. Investors might be worried about [AI spending](https://www.barrons.com/articles/meta-earnings-stock-price-e4061ff7?mod=article_inline), but analysts took their annual earnings estimates up after the quarter.

Shares of [Dow, Inc.](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/dow?mod=article_chiclet) and [ConocoPhillips](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/cop?mod=article_chiclet) qualify as unfairly punished, but those two are at the mercy of oil prices. Coming into the week, Dow stock was up 31% since fighting began in Iran. Conoco stock was up 9%.

The dozen stocks listed, excluding [ServiceNow](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/now?mod=article_chiclet), which doesn’t have rising estimates, dropped an average of about 5% after posting an average earnings beat of 23%.

That’s quite a spread, and maybe an opportunity for investors.

(The most punished was Netflix with a 62% quarterly earnings beat and a 10% drop for a spread of about 72 points.)

Of course, as always, [a screen](https://www.barrons.com/topics/barrons-stock-screen) is only the start of an investment process. After identifying ideas, comes the harder work of figuring out what’s next for each stock.

These stocks made moves:

[Apple](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/aapl?mod=article_chiclet) rose 3.3% after the iPhone maker [topped analysts’ earnings and revenue targets](https://www.barrons.com/livecoverage/apple-earnings-stock-price-iphones-ai-china-memory?mod=article_inline) for its fiscal second quarter. Revenue for the iPhone grew 22% year over year. Apple’s guidance for the current quarter also looked strong despite component shortages and high prices.

[Intel](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/intc?mod=article_chiclet) jumped 5.4% to $99.61, earlier hitting $100.45 a share intraday. Tigress Financial raised its price target to $118, up from $66 previously, on Friday. The stock is coming off its [best month of the past 30 years](https://www.barrons.com/articles/intel-is-trading-at-par-where-it-goes-next-731bf8b4?mod=article_inline), according to FactSet.

[Sandisk](https://www.barrons.com/market-data/stocks/sndk?mod=article_chiclet) advanced 8.3%. After the flash memory maker [posted stellar results](https://www.barrons.com/articles/sandisk-earnings-stock-price-02b9f821?mod=article_inline) for the fiscal third quarter, investors likely were taking the opportunity to lock in some profit, seeing as the stock has gained more than 3,000% over the past year amid the artificial-intelligence boom.
