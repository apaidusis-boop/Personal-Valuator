# GFC 2007-2009 — Global Financial Crisis

## A história em 5 fases

### Fase 1 — Build-up (2001-2006)
- Greenspan cuts pós dot-com: Fed funds 6% → 1% (2001-2003)
- **Housing bubble** inflates — prices up 50%+ em 5 anos
- Subprime lending explodes ($30B 2001 → $600B 2005)
- **Securitization** — MBS, CDOs, synthetic CDOs
- AAA ratings dadas to junk tranches

### Fase 2 — Cracks emerge (2007)
- **Feb 2007**: HSBC announces $10B subprime loss
- **Apr 2007**: New Century Financial files BK
- **Jun 2007**: Bear Stearns hedge funds collapse
- **Aug 2007**: BNP Paribas freezes 3 funds — start of credit freeze
- Fed cuts: 5.25% → 4.75%

### Fase 3 — Bear Stearns & buildup (2008 Mar)
- **Mar 14 2008**: **Bear Stearns collapse** — JPM buys $2/share (previously $170 in 2007)
- Fed orchestrates rescue ($30B loan facility)
- Fed funds cuts continue: → 2.25%
- Market assumes "the worst is over"

### Fase 4 — **Lehman collapse** (Sep-Oct 2008)
**Sep 15 2008**: Lehman Brothers files Chapter 11 — **biggest BK ever** ($600B assets).

- Credit markets freeze totalmente
- **AIG** bailed $85B (insurance collapse)
- **TARP** $700B voted Oct 3 (was defeated Sep 29, equity crash −9%)
- Fed funds: 2.00% → 0.25% em 3 meses
- First **QE1** announced Nov 2008

### Fase 5 — Bottom + recovery (2009)
- **S&P bottom**: **Mar 9 2009 @ 676** (−57% from Oct 2007 peak 1565)
- Fed commits to "zero bound indefinido"
- Stress tests em banks Mar-May 2009
- Recovery rally begins — S&P doubles by 2011

## Lições permanentes

### 1. Leverage kills
Lehman: **30:1 leverage**. One bad week = wiped out. Regulators responded: Basel III Tier 1 > 10%, CET1 requirements.

### 2. Liquidity assumptions fail in panic
- MMFs assumed liquid → "break the buck" (Reserve Primary Sep 2008)
- REPO market froze overnight
- Collateral chains broke

### 3. Correlations → 1 em crisis
Diversification myth — all risky assets crashed together Mar 2009.

### 4. Dividend cuts happen even in blue-chips
- GE cut div 2009 (30y increase streak broken)
- Bank of America cut
- Wells Fargo cut
- Even "safe" financials could not escape

### 5. Fed intervention works (arguably)
- QE + ZIRP prevented Great Depression 2.0
- But created inequality, asset inflation problems later
- **Moral hazard** increased (TBTF banks grew)

## Regulatory response

### Dodd-Frank Act (2010)
- Volcker Rule (prop trading ban)
- CFPB (consumer protection)
- Stress tests (SCAP, CCAR, DFAST)
- Living wills for TBTF banks
- Derivatives central clearing

### Basel III (2010+)
- Tier 1 capital minimum ↑
- Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR)
- Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR)

## For investor lens today

### Signals que começam parecer 2007
- Cycle peak signals
- Inverted yield curve (we have)
- Credit spreads widening
- Non-bank lending boom (private credit 2024)

### Differences today (hopefully)
- Banks mais capitalised
- Housing stock constrained (no oversupply)
- Consumer balance sheet better
- But: **sovereign debt stress** (US debt/GDP 120%, BR 80%)

## BR perspectivas

BR **passou relativamente bem** pelo GFC:
- IBOV: 73k (May 2008) → **29k** (Oct 2008) → 70k (Dec 2010)
- Fiscal stimulus from Lula govt
- Selic cut 13.75% → 8.75% → help recovery
- Commodities crashed mas recovered quick

Brazilian investor lesson: correlação com US é alta em tail events (not escape hatch).
