# Fed Funds — História 1954-2026

## Fase 1954-1965 — Establishment

- Fed funds target **emerges** post-Treasury Accord 1951
- Typical range 1-4% throughout 1950s-early 60s
- **Goldilocks era** pre-Great Inflation

## Fase 1965-1980 — "Great Inflation"

### Inflação chega
- Johnson "guns and butter" (Vietnam + Great Society) = fiscal expansion
- Oil embargo 1973 + 1979 → supply shocks
- Nixon ends gold standard 1971 → inflation unchained

### Fed fails
- Arthur Burns (1970-1978) dovish demais → inflation 8-12%
- G. William Miller (1978-1979) disaster — inflation spiked
- Stagflation stuck

## **Fase 1979-1987 — Volcker**

**Paul Volcker** assume Fed (Aug 1979). Decisão historic:

### Shock therapy (1980-1982)
- Fed funds: **6% → 20%** em 3 anos
- Fed funds **peak 20%** (Jun 1981) 🔥
- Prime rate 21.5%, 30y mortgage 18%!
- **2 recessões** 1980 + 1981-82
- Unemployment peak 10.8%

### Resultado
- CPI: 13% (1980) → 3.5% (1983) → anchored
- Stock market: bottom Aug 1982 → next 18-year bull market
- **Volcker = credibility maximum** for Fed

## Fase 1987-2006 — Greenspan

### "The Maestro"
- Fed funds oscillated 3-8%
- Era prominente de "soft landings" (1994-95, 1998-99)
- Market-friendly reputation

### Crises handled
- 1987 crash Oct 19 — cut rates immediately, no recession
- 1998 LTCM blow-up — cut + coordinate rescue
- 2000 dot-com bust — cuts 6% → 1% fast

### Critics
- Accused creating asset bubbles
- "Greenspan put" encorajou moral hazard
- 2001-2003 rates **too low too long** (1%) → housing bubble seeds

## Fase 2006-2014 — Bernanke (Great Recession)

### Pre-GFC
- Bernanke took over 2006
- Raised rates to 5.25% peak Jul 2006

### GFC (2008-2009)
- Sep 2008: Lehman collapse
- Fed funds: **5.25% → 0.25%** em 15 meses (Dec 2008)
- **Zero Lower Bound** reached
- Launched **QE1** (Nov 2008), **QE2** (Nov 2010), **QE3** (Sep 2012)
- Balance sheet: $900B (pre-crisis) → $4.5T (2014)

### Lições
- Fed acted far more aggressively than BoJ in 1990s
- Probably prevented Great Depression 2.0
- But created asset inflation in equity / real estate

## Fase 2014-2019 — Yellen / Powell Hike Cycle

### Lift-off
- Yellen era (2014-2018)
- Dec 2015: **first hike since 2006** (0.25% → 0.5%)
- Gradual quarterly hikes 2016-2018

### Powell takes over (2018)
- Trump appointment
- Continued hiking: peak **2.5%** (Dec 2018)
- Dec 2018 market crash → Powell pivot Jan 2019
- Cuts 2019: 2.5% → 1.75% (insurance cuts)

## Fase 2020-2022 — COVID shock + recovery

### March 2020 emergency
- Fed funds: **1.75% → 0.25%** in **2 weeks**
- **QE infinity** + facilities alphabet (MLF, SMCCF, etc.)
- Balance sheet $4T → **$9T**

### Stay-at-zero
- 2020-2022 Fed held 0% + QE continued
- Inflation rising end 2021 → Fed called "transitory"
- By Q1 2022 clear mistake — hike pivot

## Fase 2022-2024 — Fastest hike cycle ever

| Data | Fed funds | Action |
|---|:-:|---|
| Mar 2022 | 0-0.25% | **First hike** (25bp) |
| May 2022 | 0.75-1.00% | 50bp |
| Jun 2022 | 1.50-1.75% | **75bp** (first since 1994) |
| Jul 2022 | 2.25-2.50% | 75bp |
| Sep 2022 | 3.00-3.25% | 75bp |
| Nov 2022 | 3.75-4.00% | 75bp |
| Dec 2022 | 4.25-4.50% | 50bp |
| Feb-May 2023 | 5.00-5.25% | 3 x 25bp |
| Jul 2023 | **5.25-5.50%** 🔥 | Final hike |

**525bp in 15 months** — fastest pace since Volcker!

### Market impact
- S&P 2022: -20% (correction to bear)
- Nasdaq 2022: -33%
- Treasury AGG: -17% (worst bond year ever)
- Housing: 30y mortgage 3% → 7%+

## Fase 2024-2026 — Cuts begin

### Q4 2024 pivot
- Inflation cooled mid-2024 (~2.5% PCE)
- **Sep 2024**: **50bp cut** (first since 2020) — 5.50% → 5.00%
- Nov 2024: 25bp cut
- Dec 2024: 25bp cut → **4.50%**

### 2025 path
- Gradual 25bp cuts each meeting or skip
- End 2025: ~4.00-4.25%
- Political pressure Trump admin — cut faster?

### 2026 actual
- Current ~4.25-4.50% (placeholder — confirm)
- "Higher for longer" persists
- Markets price terminal ~3-3.5% by 2027

## Implication for equity investors

### Peak Fed tightening signal
- **Historical**: S&P bottoms **6-12 months AFTER** final hike
- 2023 final hike Jul → S&P bottomed Oct 2023
- Good BUY signal post-pivot

### Duration sensitivity
- Growth tech: sensitive (high duration CF)
- Value/div: less sensitive (near-term CF)
- Banks: positive NIM in rate cycle peak

### QE vs QT impact
- QE 2009-2014: equity ^40%/y
- QT 2018: mild equity drag
- QE COVID: equity +50% in 18m
- QT 2022+: drag clear, helped bear market
