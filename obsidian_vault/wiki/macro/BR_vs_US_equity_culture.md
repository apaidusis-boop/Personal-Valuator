# 🇧🇷🇺🇸 Equity culture — BR vs US structural comparison

## Macro pane — por que divergem

### Juros real secular

**BR**: juros real historicamente 4-6%+ post-Plano Real (1994). Selic nominal 10-15% frequente, IPCA 4-6%, real positivo notável.

**US**: juros real ~0-2% long-term (Fed funds - CPI). Real negativo 2020-2022 unprecedented.

**Implicação**:
- BR: renda fixa genuinely competitiva com equity. "6% DY" em BR competing with 11% CDI → choose cautious.
- US: 10Y yields 4% ano-2024-25 = unusual vs 2010-2021 0-2%. Equity risk premium compressed.

### Inflação estrutural

**BR**: 4-6% band target, 6-9% volatile (2016-2023).
**US**: 2% target, core 3-5% recent post-COVID.

**Effect em equity**:
- BR equities precisam outperformar inflação consistente para preservar real wealth.
- Taxas nominais altas valorizam dividend yield (cash flow > growth discounting).

### Currency risk (exporters vs domestic)

**BR equity divisão**:
- **BRL producers**: BBAS3, bancos, utilities domestic → full BRL exposure.
- **USD producers**: VALE3, PETR4 (parcial), SUZB3 (export) → natural USD hedge.

**BR consumer portfolio**:
- Allocate 40-60% BRL (bancos, utilities, consumo), 40-60% USD-linked (US stocks, US ADRs).

**US equity**: internal. S&P 500 40%+ revenue external, so USD depreciation helps earnings. But home currency = USD by definition, so no "currency risk" at portfolio level for US residents.

## Tax landscape driving behavior

### BR structural advantage: dividend isenção

See [[BR_dividend_isencao]] — **0%** tax on dividends for residents:
- BR ITUB4 8% DY → 8% net.
- US MSFT 0.7% DY → 0.7% × 85% (15% qualified tax) = 0.59% net.

Result: BR portfolio optimizes for **cash yield**. US portfolio optimizes for **total return** (cap gain + buyback).

### US structural advantage: buybacks

- Buybacks reduce share count → EPS ↑ mechanically.
- No immediate tax event for holder (only on sale).
- Tax-deferred compounding.
- AAPL, MSFT, GOOG reduced shares 10-25% past decade via buyback.

BR companies:
- Traditionally **payout dividendo** não buyback.
- Law prior 2000s limited buybacks.
- Emerging 2020+ (Itau, B3, Ambev) but minor vs dividend.

## Aristocrat culture

### US Aristocrats (25y+ streak) / Kings (50y+)
- **Structural cultural obsession** com streak. CEOs prioritise holding streak.
- ~65 Aristocrats current. ~35 Kings.
- PG 68y, KO 62y, CL 61y, JNJ 62y, HRL 58y, etc.
- Part of "compact" investor-corporate: "pay me, I'll forgive bumps".

### BR equivalent — nascent
- ITUB4 (bancos pagam div há décadas, but variable amount).
- ABEV3 divide irregular special.
- TAEE11 relatively steady (utilities).
- **No formal "Aristocrat" list** por structural reason — payout ratios variam cycle-to-cycle.

→ BR Aristocrat concept **doesn't map cleanly**. Use [[Dividend_Safety]] score instead.

## Retail participation

### US
- 58% households own stocks directly or via 401k/IRA.
- 401k tax-deferred → massive passive flows structural.
- ETF AUM $8T+ US.
- Robinhood gamification 2020-21 meme stocks phenomenon.

### BR
- ~5-6M individual investors active B3 (out of 215M).
- ~12% population with brokerage account (many idle).
- Tesouro Direto IPO-scale growth 2015-2022.
- FIIs surge 2019-2021 democratised real estate.
- **Financial literacy gap** significant.

**Implication for behavior**:
- US equity prices more retail-driven in specific segments (meme, ARK).
- BR pricing institutional-dominant (pension funds, foreign $).

## Corporate governance

### US
- SOX 2002 post-Enron.
- Dodd-Frank 2010 post-GFC.
- PCAOB independent audit oversight.
- SEC enforcement ~~2x BR CVM ratio.
- Class action culture.
- Board independence norms.

### BR
- **Novo Mercado** (2000) — B3 tier voluntary governance standards.
- Níveis 1/2/Novo Mercado/Tradicional listing tiers.
- Tag-along 100% Novo Mercado (minority protection).
- Ethics case by case (Petrobrás scandal, JBS, Odebrecht).
- Enforcement CVM cresce mas lento.

**Signal for investor**: prefer **Novo Mercado** listed BR names when possible. All our holdings BR are NM.

## Currency thesis — BRL secular weakening

History 1994-2026:
- 1994 Plano Real: R$ 1.00 = $1.
- 2002: R$ 4.00.
- 2008: R$ 1.60.
- 2011: R$ 1.55.
- 2015: R$ 4.20.
- 2020: R$ 5.80.
- 2025-2026: R$ 5.60-6.00.

**Secular depreciation** ~3-5%/y average.
**Drivers**: terms of trade, interest differential, fiscal risk.

**Implication for PF BR**:
- Holding 100% BR assets = **pure BRL exposure**.
- Holding 30-50% US equity = partial USD protection.
- VALE3/PETR4/SUZB3 as USD-linked BR assets (commodity export, price in USD).
- [[BRL_USD_PTAX]] = the most important single variable for portfolio BR.

## Practical trading differences

### Liquidez
- US mega-caps (MSFT, AAPL): billions in daily volume. Spread 1 basis point.
- BR blue-chips (VALE3, ITUB4): millions in daily volume. Spread 5-15 bp.
- US small/mid cap: variable.
- BR small cap: **illiquid often**, spread 30-100 bp possible.

### Trading hours
- US: 9:30-16:00 ET, extended 4-20:00 ET (limited).
- BR: 10:00-17:30 BRT, after-market 17:30-18:55 BRT.
- Overlap 10:30-16:00 BRT (= 9:30-15:00 ET) — full market alignment window.

### Commissions
- US: $0 brokers common (Robinhood, Fidelity, Schwab, IB).
- BR: R$ 0 emerging (XP, Clear, Inter) but legacy brokers charge R$ 10-20.
- Corretagem + emolumentos + ISS ~R$ 4-8 typical por trade BR.

### Taxes
- BR: DARF monthly if > R$ 35k month. Auto by broker (some).
- US resident BR: complicated. See [[Dividend_withholding_BR_US]].

## Investment styles — culture fit

### BR-style DRIP (income-focused)
- Heavy dividend + FIIs.
- Benefit from isenção.
- Low turnover.
- [[Buffett_quality]] framework.

### US-style Compounder (growth-focused)
- Low dividend + high ROIC.
- Benefit from buybacks + tax deferral.
- Moderate turnover (trim winners).
- [[Moat_types]] analysis.

Our portfolio combines both.

## Red flags — cross-market mistakes

- **Applying BR DRIP thinking to US pure growth names** (NVDA has negligible DY — can't DRIP).
- **Applying US Aristocrat framework to BR** (structural payout irregularity).
- **Ignoring currency in return calculation** (10%/y BR equity = maybe 5%/y USD terms).
- **Tax arbitrage assumptions** (BR dividend 0% tax today may change with PL 1.087).
