# 🎯 Thesis: [[BN]] — Brookfield Corporation

> Global alternatives asset manager + permanent capital holding company. **Post-2023 split**: BN = parent (distributes owns + asset mgmt fees); BAM = publicly traded asset manager pure-play.

## Intent
**Growth** — capital gain driver, NOT DRIP. DY baixo (0.6%) deliberate — reinvestment superior aos dividendos.

## Business snapshot

Post-December 2022 split:
- **BN (parent / holdco)**: permanent capital + ownership stakes em operating businesses.
  - Owns ~75% [[BAM]] (listed asset manager).
  - Direct owner of real estate, renewables, infrastructure, insurance (Brookfield Reinsurance), private equity.
- **BAM (subsidiary listed separately)**: pure fee-based asset manager, $1T+ AUM.

Segments operating:
- Renewable Power (BEPC)
- Infrastructure (BIPC)
- Real Estate
- Private Equity (BBU)
- Insurance (Brookfield Reinsurance)

## Por que detemos

1. **Bruce Flatt leadership** — capital allocator class.
2. **Hard asset exposure** (infra, energy transition, RE) with insurance float leverage.
3. **Compounding machine** — 15-20%/y TSR target long-term.
4. **Permanent capital** — NOT subject to fund redemptions (unlike pure PE peers).
5. **Fee-stream BAM** upside vs owning BN discount.
6. **Energy transition secular** — renewables + grid (Brookfield owns GB Energy + Westinghouse).

## Moat

- **Scale** — $1T AUM raises funds no competitor can match em alternatives.
- **Deal sourcing** — proprietary origination relationships 40+ years.
- **Permanent capital** structure stable vs cyclical PE.
- **Insurance float** at Brookfield Reinsurance = long-dated liabilities paired with hard assets.

## Current state (2026-04)

- Renewable power theme strong (AI datacenter power demand ramping).
- Infra (BIP/BIPC) — growing dividend stream.
- Real estate (office segment problematic — Brookfield Property held some legacy office).
- Asset management fees growing double-digit yearly.
- Insurance growing aggressively (BAM Re, Brookfield Reinsurance).

## Invalidation triggers

- [ ] Major capital mis-allocation (large write-down > $5B)
- [ ] Flatt departure sem succession (Connor Teskey seen as heir)
- [ ] Fund raising cycles break (AUM growth stalls)
- [ ] Leverage ratio at parent > 3× credit concern
- [ ] Real estate portfolio major impairment (ongoing watch)
- [ ] Capital recycling slows (execution risk)

## Sizing

- Posição actual: 7 shares
- Target 2-3% sleeve US
- **Growth, not DRIP** — no auto-reinvest rationale
- Add on dips (-15% from 52w high)

## BN vs BAM trade-off

| Trait | BN (parent) | BAM (listed sub) |
|---|---|---|
| Exposure | Owns + fees + operating biz | Pure fee-based |
| DY | 0.6% | 3.5% |
| Complexity | High (multiple tiers) | Low (clean asset manager) |
| Growth | 15-20%/y target | 10-15%/y target |
| Valuation | Sum-of-parts discount | Clean P/E |

Hold **BN** if you want full Brookfield exposure. Hold **BAM** if you want clean fee-income. We hold BN (upside + insurance + hard assets).
