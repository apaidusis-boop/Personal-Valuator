# 🌲 Pulp Cycle — framework

## Fases canónicas

```
Tight Supply → Capacity Adding → Oversupply → Discipline → Tight
   │              │                  │            │           │
BEKP $800+     BEKP $650-750      BEKP $450-550  BEKP $550-700  BEKP $750+
```

## Fase 1: Tight supply

**Sinais**:
- Pulp spot prices (CFR China BEKP) > $800/t
- Inventory at Chinese ports < 1.5Mt
- Producers increase dividend + buyback
- New fab announcements multi-billion

**Duration**: 12-24 meses.
**Winners**: Pure-play pulp (SUZB3 > KLBN4).
**Action**: **TRIM** em peak. **Historical SUZB peak**: 2018 ($R 58), 2022 ($R 60+).

**Historical peaks**:
- 2011 ($850)
- 2018 ($820)
- 2022 ($850+)
- 2025 ($750+ ongoing)

## Fase 2: Capacity adding

**Sinais**:
- New mills completing (2-3y post-announcement)
- Suzano Cerrado 2024 (+2.5Mt) — major supply addition
- Arauco MAPA Chile — competing addition
- Spot prices plateau or retreat from peak

**Duration**: 6-18 meses.
**Winners**: None outperform. Integrated players (KLBN4) less hit.
**Action**: **Reduce exposure** pure-play pulp.

## Fase 3: Oversupply

**Sinais**:
- Inventory buildups at ports (China > 2.2Mt)
- Spot prices < $500/t (stress marginal producers)
- BR mills near break-even cash
- Nordic + Chile mills breakeven higher → closures

**Duration**: 6-12 meses.
**Winners**: Lowest-cost (SUZB3 cash cost $180/t) survive.
**Losers**: High-cost Nordic (NBSK ~$350/t).

**Historical troughs**:
- 2009 ($450)
- 2016 ($430)
- 2020 ($450 COVID)

## Fase 4: Discipline / recovery

**Sinais**:
- Supply curtailment announced
- Inventory normalizing (< 1.8Mt ports)
- Capex cuts from peers
- Spot prices rising slowly

**Duration**: 12-18 meses.
**Winners**: BR low-cost + vertically integrated (SUZB3, KLBN4).
**Action**: **Accumulate** em trough.

## BR advantage estrutural

BEKP (eucalipto) cash cost por t:
- **BR (SUZB3, KLBN4, Bracell): $170-220/t**
- North American hardwood: $350-450/t
- Nordic NBSK: $400-500/t

**Why**:
- Eucalipto cresce 7 anos BR vs 25 anos pinho NHem.
- Plantação: 1 hectare gera 7x mais fibra/ano que Nordic softwood.
- Low-cost labor, land, energy.

**Consequence**: BR mills operate profitable em $400-450/t. Nordic loses money. Global pulp industry reshape toward BR dominance.

## FX como driver complementar

USD/BRL relação:
- 100% SUZB3 revenue em USD (export).
- 80% SUZB3 cost em BRL (plantação, labor, energy, logistics BR).
- **$1 BRL weaker = ~2-3% EBITDA impact** favorável.

PTAX:
- BRL forte (R$ 4.50) = margin compression.
- BRL fraco (R$ 5.50-6.00) = margin windfall.
- **Historical**: 2020 BRL R$ 5.80 + BEKP $450 = bad. 2022 BRL R$ 5.20 + BEKP $850 = SUZB3 EBITDA record.

Double lever → maximum variance.

## Downstream dynamics

**Pulp → Paper split**:
- 60% pulp goes to tissue (growing 3-5%/y).
- 25% packaging cardboard (growing 5-10%/y, e-commerce).
- 15% printing paper (secular decline -3 a -5%/y).

**Long-term thesis pro-pulp**:
- Tissue demand grows EM structurally.
- Packaging cardboard grows e-commerce.
- Printing decline manageable.
- Eucalipto BR structural advantage persists.

## Red flags — cycle turning bearish

- China inventory > 2.3Mt sustained
- 3+ major capacity additions announced same year
- China PMI manufacturing < 48
- USD/BRL strengthening to R$ 4.50

## Red flags — cycle turning bullish

- Inventory < 1.5Mt (acute tight)
- Nordic mill closures announced
- Spot prices rising 10%+ MoM
- USD/BRL R$ 5.50+ (tailwind)

## Playbook application

- **Fase 1 (Tight)**: **TRIM** SUZB3, keep KLBN4.
- **Fase 2 (Capacity adding)**: **reduce** pulp exposure.
- **Fase 3 (Oversupply)**: **accumulate** quality (SUZB3 low-cost).
- **Fase 4 (Recovery)**: **ADD**.
