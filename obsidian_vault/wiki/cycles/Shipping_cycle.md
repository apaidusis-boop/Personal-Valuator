## Fases canónicas (tanker cycle)

```
Trough → Recovery → Boom → Peak → Correction → Trough
  │         │        │      │        │
Rates      Rates     Rates  Rates   Rates
low        rising   high    peaking  falling
($10-15k)  ($25-40k) ($50-80k) ($60-90k)$25-35k)
```

Rates em USD/day para VLCC (Very Large Crude Carrier).

## Fase 1: Trough

**Sinais**:
- Rates $10-15k/day VLCC (below opex ~$20k).
- Newbuild orders at 5-year low.
- Scrapping activity high (older tonnage).
- Yards competing for few orders.

**Duration**: 12-24 meses.
**Winners**: **Buyers of distressed tonnage** (cycles 2002-03, 2012-13, 2020).
**Action**: Accumulate quality operators.

**Historical troughs**:
- 2002 (post-dotcom)
- 2013 (post-oversupply)
- 2020 (COVID demand collapse)

## Fase 2: Recovery

**Sinais**:
- Rates $25-40k/day.
- Ships redeploying favoravelmente.
- Scrapping moderating.
- Newbuild orders restrained (owners cautious).
- Fleet age increasing (limited newbuilds 2014-2022).

**Duration**: 18-36 meses.
**Winners**: Operators with fleets > 10 ships.
**Action**: **Hold**, accumulate balance sheet.

## Fase 3: Boom

**Sinais**:
- Rates $50-80k/day.
- Record dividends declared (tanker owners pay cash generous).
- Newbuild ordering activity surging (yards booked 2-3y out).
- Ships value soaring (asset-backed leverage).
- Special dividends signature.

**Duration**: 12-24 meses.
**Winners**: Anyone with operational fleet.
**Action**: **TRIM** near peak. Watch newbuild orderbook > 10% fleet.

**Historical booms**:
- 2004-2005 (pre-GFC commodity supercycle)
- 2007-2008 (peak supercycle)
- 2022-2023 (Russia war, ton-mile shift Black Sea → Asia routes)
- 2024-2025 continuing (supply discipline + Russia shifts)

## Fase 4: Peak → Correction

**Sinais**:
- Rates $60-90k/day sustained.
- Newbuild orderbook > 10% existing fleet (suggesting supply response 18-24m out).
- Ship values at decade highs (~2× scrap value → overextended).
- Owner behavior shift: dividend cuts OR **asset sales** (selling ships to cash out).
- **Deadweight ton-miles stagnating**.

**Duration**: 6-18 meses.
**Losers**: High-beta distressed (marginal operators).
**Action**: **SELL / AVOID** new positions.

**Historical peak-to-correction episodes**:
- 2008 → 2009 (GFC crushed rates -80% in 6 months)
- 2015 → 2016 (Saudi oversupply)
- 2026 (projected → see [[TEN]] memo)

## Drivers of demand

### Oil tanker
- Global crude production + refined products consumption.
- **Ton-miles** = tonnage × distance (longer routes = more demand).
- Russia war 2022 shifted routes Urals-Asia (longer than Urals-Europe) → ton-miles spike.
- China SPR (strategic petroleum reserve) filling adds.
- EV penetration LONG-TERM headwind (10+ years).

### Dry bulk
- Iron ore (China demand #1 driver).
- Coal (China + India).
- Grains (seasonal).

### Container
- Consumer goods + electronics global.
- 2021-2022 COVID reopening boom.
- 2023-2024 oversupply correction.

## Supply constraints

Newbuilding shipyard capacity:
- Korean (Hyundai, Samsung, DSME) + Chinese (Hudong, NSS, YZJ) + Japanese.
- Yard slots booked 2-3y ahead in boom.
- Lead time order → delivery = 2-3 years typical.
- **Implication**: supply response delayed = cycle extreme.

Scrapping:
- Ship lifespan ~20-25 years tanker.
- Recycling at Alang/Chittagong/Turkey.
- Scrap prices correlate with steel demand.

- Rates at cash break-even < 12 months
- Geopolitics disrupting routes (Russia, Iran)
- Commodity super-cycle evidence (2021-22 style)
