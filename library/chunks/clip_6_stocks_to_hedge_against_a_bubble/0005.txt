## 5\. WASTE MANAGEMENT (NYSE: WM)

In a world chasing breakthroughs and moonshots, Waste Management is built on something far more reliable: essential services with steady, recurring demand.

The company is the largest provider of waste collection, disposal, and recycling services in North America. It serves municipalities, businesses, and households through long-term contracts, many of which include built-in price escalators that adjust with inflation.

That gives Waste Management a powerful advantage: steady, predictable cash flow in just about every economic environment.

It also benefits from a unique form of protection. Waste is a local business, heavily regulated and deeply embedded in local infrastructure. That creates high barriers to entry and gives WM something rare in any industry: territorial dominance.

But this isn’t just a legacy trash hauler. Over the past few years, Waste Management has invested heavily in automation, building high-efficiency recycling facilities that generate stronger margins than their manual counterparts. It’s also expanding into renewable energy by capturing landfill gas and converting it into fuel.

It’s worth noting that it carries debt. And yes, parts of the business, especially commercial and construction-related activity, can be cyclical. But Waste Management’s core services are essential and inelastic. People don’t cancel trash service when the economy slows.

As long as people still have trash to throw away, Waste Management will stay in demand.
