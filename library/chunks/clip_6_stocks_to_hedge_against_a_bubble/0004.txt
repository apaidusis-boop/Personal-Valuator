## 4\. CASEY’S GENERAL STORE (NASDAQ: CASY)

If you’ve ever driven through small-town America, you’ve likely stumbled across a Casey’s.

The company operates nearly 3,000 convenience stores, serving up fuel, groceries, and grab-and-go food to communities often overlooked by bigger national chains. And it’s not just selling snacks and gas; Casey’s is the fifth-largest pizza chain in the U.S.

That food business is a quiet profit engine. Prepared food and beverage margins have recently topped 50%, helping to support healthy overall returns even when fuel margins compress.

Casey’s has also grown steadily through smart, well-priced acquisitions. In late 2024, Casey’s purchased nearly 200 CEFCO gas stations across multiple states, continuing its steady expansion without overreaching. The company plans to open at least 80 more stores in fiscal 2026, keeping growth focused and manageable.

Unlike many retailers, Casey’s owns much of its supply chain, including three major distribution centers and a majority of its fuel delivery trucks. That vertical integration gives it better cost control, faster response times, and more flexibility to reinvest in customer experience.

Since 1990, Casey’s stock has outperformed the S&P 500 by a factor of nearly 8 to 1. That kind of compounding comes not from hype, but from a business model that works, and keeps working.
