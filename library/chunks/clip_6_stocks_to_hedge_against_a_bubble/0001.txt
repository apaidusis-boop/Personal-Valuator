## 1\. COSTCO (NASDAQ: COST)

Costco is the kind of business that just works.

It delivers low prices, loyal customers, and consistent results. Memberships provide access to deeply discounted groceries, household goods, and everyday essentials. And through good economies and bad, the model holds up.

While AI companies grab headlines with vaporware and burn rates, Costco sells more than 150 million rotisserie chickens a year and generates billions in membership fees alone. That’s the kind of recurring, high-margin revenue most software startups would kill for.

Renewal rates in the U.S. exceed 90 percent. Globally, they’re close. That kind of stickiness is rare in retail, and it reflects the trust Costco has built by passing savings directly to members. The company famously caps most markups at around 14 to 15 percent, far below industry averages.
