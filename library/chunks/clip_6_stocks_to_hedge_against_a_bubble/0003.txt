## 3\. STARBUCKS (NASDAQ: SBUX)

Starbucks has become part of daily life for millions of people around the world.

With more than 18,000 stores in North America and a growing international footprint, it’s one of the most recognizable and trusted brands in modern retail. But Starbucks’ real strength isn’t just its scale; it’s the loyalty and routine it builds with customers.

For many, a stop at Starbucks isn’t a splurge. It’s a habit. And that habit drives consistent traffic, pricing power, and reliable revenue across economic cycles.

Under CEO Brian Niccol, the company has been refocusing on what made the brand successful in the first place. That’s meant simplifying the menu, doubling down on in-store experience, and restoring Starbucks’ identity as a welcoming, high-touch “third place” between home and work.

Niccol has also shown a willingness to make hard decisions including closing underperforming stores, retraining teams, and streamlining operations. At the same time, Starbucks continues to invest in digital ordering, loyalty programs, and high-growth international markets like China and India.
