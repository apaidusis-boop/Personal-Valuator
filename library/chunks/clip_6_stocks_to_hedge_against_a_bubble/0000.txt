When the CEO of OpenAI, a legendary hedge fund billionaire, and the Fed Chair all start hinting at the same thing… we pay attention.

Sam Altman, who helped spark this entire boom, recently said:

*“Are we in a phase where investors as a whole are overexcited about AI? My opinion is yes.”*

Ray Dalio, founder of Bridgewater Associates, sees it too, calling this moment “very similar” to the dotcom bubble of the late 90s and early 2000s.

Even Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell recently acknowledged that:

*“Equity prices are fairly highly valued.”*

For a Fed Chair, that’s as close to “careful now” as it gets.

And behind the scenes? Meta is pausing AI hiring. MIT says 95% of GenAI projects are failing inside companies.

Even Google DeepMind’s co-founder said the explosion of funding into generative AI is starting to look a lot like the crypto boom, complete with inflated expectations.

**We don’t bring all this up to scare you out of AI.**

Far from it.

We believe AI is one of the most pivotal, transformational technologies of our lifetimes.

That’s why we’ve published multiple in-depth reports on AI. Why we’ve recommended companies like Nvidia, Alphabet, AMD, and ASML, years before AI was mainstream.

But even with all that conviction, we also believe in staying grounded, especially when the market starts leaning too far in one direction.

As Motley Fool CEO Tom Gardner recently said in a quarterly call:

*“Even artificial intelligence, as with every other innovation in history, can become overvalued”*

He’s also pointed out that today’s valuations across many U.S. equities are running rich, and that now is a good moment to pause, assess, and in his words, *“take one step closer toward risk management.”*

That doesn’t mean abandoning innovation.

It means **diversifying your holdings and investing across multiple sectors**, so your portfolio is positioned for the future, without being overexposed to a single theme.

That’s what this report is about.
