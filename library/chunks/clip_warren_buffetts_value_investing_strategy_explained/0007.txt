The liquidation value doesn't include intangibles that aren't directly stated on the financial statements, such as the value of a brand name. When Buffett determines the company's intrinsic value as a whole, he compares it to its current market capitalization: its current total worth or price.19

Sounds easy, doesn't it? Buffett's success depends on his unmatched skill in accurately determining this intrinsic value. We can outline some of his criteria and mimic his strategies, but we have no way of knowing if we can be as successful as he is.
