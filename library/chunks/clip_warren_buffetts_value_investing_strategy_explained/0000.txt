### Key Takeaways

- Warren Buffett utilizes value investing focused on undervalued securities.
- He looks at entire companies, not just stock market intricacies.
- Buffett's investment portfolio includes Apple, American Express, and Coca-Cola.
- His first rule of investing is to never lose money.
- Buffett emphasizes investing in oneself for financial success.

Warren Buffett is known as both a savvy businessman and a generous philanthropist. He's one of the world's richest people, consistently ranking high on the Forbes list of billionaires. His net worth was listed at a bit more than $142.3 billion on March 4, 2026.1

He's probably best known for being one of the world's most successful investors. Buffett's investment strategy has reached mythical proportions. He subscribes to several important tenets and an investment philosophy that's widely followed around the globe. So, what are the secrets to his success?

Buffett has a careful methodology for evaluating stocks and investing, and he shares some tips.

## The History of Warren Buffett

[Warren Buffett](https://www.investopedia.com/warren-buffett-s-first-home-11737155) was born in Omaha, Nebraska, in 1930. He developed an interest in the business world and in investing at an early age, including the [stock market](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/s/stockmarket.asp). Buffett started his education at the Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania before moving to the University of Nebraska, where he received an undergraduate degree in business administration. He later went to the Columbia Business School, where he earned his graduate degree in economics.2

Buffett began his career as an investment salesperson in the early 1950s, and he formed Buffett Associates in 1956. He was in control of Berkshire Hathaway less than 10 years later, in 1965.3
