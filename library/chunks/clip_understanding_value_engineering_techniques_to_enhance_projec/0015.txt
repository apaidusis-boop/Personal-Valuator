## The Bottom Line

Value engineering is a systematic approach designed to maximize a product’s function while minimizing its costs. Originating at GE during World War II, it provides a structured six-phase process to enhance product value. The approach focuses on balancing cost reduction with maintaining or improving functionality, helping products better serve customers and remain competitive in the marketplace. When applied effectively, it can yield long-term savings and stronger product performance. Companies should integrate it into their development process while being mindful not to sacrifice innovation or quality for short-term savings.
