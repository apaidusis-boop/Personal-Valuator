The dividend discount model is a special case of equity valuation, where the value of equity is the
present value of expected future dividends.
The value of the firm is obtained by discounting expected cash flows to the firm (i.e., the residual
cash flows after meeting all operating expenses, reinvestment needs, and taxes, but prior to any
payments to either debt or equity holders) at the weighted average cost of capital (WACC), which is
the cost of the different components of financing used by the firm, weighted by their market value
proportions.
