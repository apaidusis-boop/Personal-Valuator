Cross-Sectional Distribution of PEG Ratios
Now that the PEG ratio has been defined, the cross-sectional distribution of PEG ratios across all
U.S. firms is examined in 
Figure 18.6
. In estimating these PEG ratios, the estimates of growth in
earnings per share over the next five years are used in conjunction with the current PEs. The
distribution of PEG ratios for the market closely resembles the distribution of PE ratios, with one
important caveat. The PEG ratio can be computed only for those firms where there is an expected
growth rate in earnings. Since this growth rate is usually provided by analysts, any firm that is not
tracked by analysts will not have a PEG ratio (at least in our sample). Since smaller, younger firms
tend not to be followed by analysts, this can create a subtle selection bias in the sample.
