Impact of Exercise on Underlying Asset Value
The Black-Scholes model is based on the assumption that exercising an option does not affect the
value of the underlying asset. This may be true for listed options on stocks, but it is not true for some
types of options. For instance, the exercise of warrants increases the number of shares outstanding
and brings fresh cash into the firm, both of which will affect the stock price.
