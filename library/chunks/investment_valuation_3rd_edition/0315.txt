Private information about the firm.
 Analysts sometimes have access to private information
about the firms they follow that may be relevant in forecasting future growth. This avoids
answering the delicate question of when private information becomes illegal inside information.
There is no doubt, however, that good private information can lead to significantly better
estimates of future growth. In an attempt to restrict this type of information leakage, the SEC
issued new regulations preventing firms from selectively revealing information to a few analysts
or investors. Outside the United States, however, firms routinely convey private information to
analysts following them.
