Firms Model Works Best For
The allowance for a gradual decrease in growth rates over time may make this a useful model for
firms that are growing rapidly right now, but where the growth is expected to decline gradually over
time as the firms get larger and the differential advantage they have over their competitors declines.
The assumption that the payout ratio is constant, however, makes this an inappropriate model to use
for any firm that has low or no dividends currently. Thus, the model, by requiring a combination of
high growth and high payout, may be quite limited
