Comparing PE Ratios across Firms in the Market
 In the preceding section, comparable firms were
narrowly defined to be other firms in the same business. This section considers ways in which we can
expand the number of comparable firms by looking at an entire sector or even the market. There are
two advantages in doing this. The first is that the estimates may become more precise as the number
of comparable firms increase. The second is that it allows you to pinpoint when firms in a small
subgroup are being under- or overvalued relative to the rest of the sector or the market. Since the
differences across firms will increase when you loosen the definition of comparable firms, you have
to adjust for these differences. The simplest way of doing this is with a multiple regression, with the
PE ratio as the dependent variable, and proxies for risk, growth, and payout forming the independent
variables.
