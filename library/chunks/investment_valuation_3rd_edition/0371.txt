Two-Stage Dividend Discount Model
The two-stage growth model allows for two stages of growth—an initial phase where the growth rate
is not a stable growth rate and a subsequent steady state where the growth rate is stable and is
expected to remain so for the long term. While, in most cases, the growth rate during the initial phase
is higher than the stable growth rate, the model can be adapted to value companies that are expected
to post low or even negative growth rates for a few years and then revert back to stable growth.
