Exxon Mobil is in stable growth with the operating income growing at 2% a year in perpetuity. New
investments are expected to generate a return on capital that reflects the normalized operating income and
current capital invested; this return on capital is used to compute a reinvestment rate.
While Exxon reported operating income of more than $60 billion in 2008, the oil price had dropped to $45 by March 2009,
