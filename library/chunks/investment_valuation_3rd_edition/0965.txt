Cross-sectional data:
 In some cases, you may be able to substitute data on differences in a
specific variable across existing investments that are similar to the investment being analyzed.
Assume that you are valuing a software firm and are concerned about the volatility in operating
margins. 
Figure 33.7
 provides a distribution of pretax operating margins across software
companies in 2011. 
In a simulation, you could either use this distribution directly or find a standardized statistical
distribution that is close in terms of characteristics. Note, though, that if you use this distribution,
you are in effect assuming that the underlying distribution of margins is the same across software
firms.
