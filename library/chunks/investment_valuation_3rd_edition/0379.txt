H Model for Valuing Growth
The H model is a two-stage model for growth, but unlike the classic two-stage model, the growth rate
in the initial growth phase is not constant but declines linearly over time to reach the stable growth
rate in steady state. This model was presented in Fuller and Hsia (1984).
