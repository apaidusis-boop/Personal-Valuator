6.

Riskless interest rate corresponding to life of the option.
 Since the buyer of an option pays the
price of the option up front, an opportunity cost is involved. This cost will depend on the level of
interest rates and the time to expiration of the option. The riskless interest rate also enters into the
valuation of options when the present value of the exercise price is calculated, since the exercise
price does not have to be paid (received) until expiration on calls (puts). Increases in the interest
rate will increase the value of calls and reduce the value of puts.
