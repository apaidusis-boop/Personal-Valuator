BACKGROUND
Lutèce was founded in 1961 by Andre Soltner, and quickly acquired a reputation for serving food of exceptional quality. It

had received a five-star rating from Mobil for 24 consecutive years and was one of five New York City restaurants that got
a four-star rating (the highest) from the 
New York Times
. In a sign of slippage, however, its ranking in the 
Zagat Survey of
New York City Restaurants
 dropped to eighth from being perennially at or near the top for much of the 1970s and 1980s.
ESTIMATING CASH FLOWS
The following are some of the background facts on Lutèce:
The restaurant can seat 92 diners. It has one seating for lunch and two seatings for dinner. It fills in 70% of
its seats at lunchtime and 80% of its seats at dinner.
The restaurant stays open 340 days every year, and is closed for the remaining 25 days.
The average price of a lunch is $30, and the average price of a dinner is $66. Approximately one-third of this
is for liquor.
There are 42 employees on the staff of the restaurant. The cost of food is approximately 30% of the price of
the meal, and the payroll amounts to $1.25 million a year.
The annual rent for the space used by Lutèce is $600,000.
