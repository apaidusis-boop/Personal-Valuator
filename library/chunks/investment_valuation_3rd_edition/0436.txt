 The interest expenses are equal to the pretax cost of debt multiplied by the market value of debt.
If a firm has old debt on its books, with interest expenses that are different from this value, the two
approaches will diverge.
If there is expected growth, the potential for inconsistency multiplies. You have to ensure that you
borrow enough money to fund new investments to keep your debt ratio at a level consistent with what
you are assuming when you compute the cost of capital.
