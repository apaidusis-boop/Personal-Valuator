Using the PEG Ratio for Comparisons
As with the PE ratio, the PEG ratio is used to compare the valuations of firms that are in the same
business. As noted in the preceding section, the PEG ratio is a function of the risk, growth potential,
and payout ratio of a firm. This section looks at ways of using the PEG ratio and examines some of
the problems in comparing PEG ratios across firms.
Direct Comparisons
Most analysts who use PEG ratios compute them for firms within a sector (or comparable firm group)
and compare these ratios. Firms with lower PEG ratios are usually viewed as undervalued, even if
growth rates are different across the firms being compared. This approach is based on the incorrect
perception that PEG ratios control for differences in growth. In fact, direct comparisons of PEG
ratios work only if firms are similar in terms of growth potential, risk, and payout ratios (or returns
on equity). If this were the case, however, you could just as easily compare PE ratios across firms.
