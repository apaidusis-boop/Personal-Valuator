Historical Risk Premiums: Other Markets
If it is difficult to estimate a reliable historical premium for the U.S. market, it becomes doubly so
when looking at markets with short and volatile histories. This is clearly true for emerging markets,
but it is also true for the European equity markets. While the economies of Germany, Italy, and France
may be mature, their equity markets do not share the same characteristic. Until two decades ago, they
tend to be dominated by a few large companies; many businesses remain private; and trading was,
except on a few stocks.
