The PE ratio is much more sensitive to changes in expected growth rates when interest rates are
low than when they are high. The reason is simple. Growth produces cash flows in the future, and the
present value of these cash flows is much smaller at high interest rates. Consequently, the effect of
changes in the growth rate on the present value tends to be smaller.
