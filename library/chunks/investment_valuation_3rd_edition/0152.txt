The size of the small firm premium, while it has varied across time, has been generally positive. It
was highest during the 1970s and early 1980s and lowest during the 1990s before returning in the first
half of the last decade. The persistence of this premium has led to several possible explanations.

1.
 The transaction costs of investing in small stocks are significantly higher than the transaction
costs of investing in larger stocks, and the premiums are estimated prior to these costs. While this
is generally true, the differential transaction costs are unlikely to explain the magnitude of the
premium across time, and are likely to become even less critical for longer investment horizons.
