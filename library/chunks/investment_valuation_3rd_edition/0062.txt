Boeing, based on its operating income in 1998, looks riskier than the Home Depot on both the interest coverage ratio basis
and fixed charges coverage ratio basis. On a cash flow basis, however, Boeing does look much better. In fact, when capital
expenditures are considered, the Home Depot has a lower ratio. For Boeing, the other consideration is the fact that operating
income in 1998 was depressed relative to income in earlier years, and this does have an impact on the ratios across the
board. It might make more sense when computing these ratios to look at the average operating income over time.
