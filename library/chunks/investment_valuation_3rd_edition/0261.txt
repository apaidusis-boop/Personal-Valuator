Thus, in the case of the research asset with a five-year life, you cumulate one-fifth of the R&D
expenses from four years ago, two-fifths of the R&D expenses from three years ago, three-fifths of the
R&D expenses from two years ago, four-fifths of the R&D expenses from last year, and this year's
entire R&D expense to arrive at the value of the research asset. This augments the value of the assets
of the firm and, by extension, the book value of equity.

operating income, reflecting their recategorization as capital expenses. Next, the amortization of the
