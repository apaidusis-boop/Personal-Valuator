A bottom-up beta can be adapted to reflect actual changes in a firm's business mix and
expected changes in the future. Thus if a firm divested a major portion of its operations last
week, the weights on the businesses can be modified to reflect the divestiture. The same can
be done with acquisitions. In fact, a firm's strategic plans to enter new businesses in the
future can be brought into the beta estimates for future periods.
