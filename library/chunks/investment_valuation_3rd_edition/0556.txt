The price–book value ratio of a stable firm is determined by the differential between the return on
equity and its cost of equity. If the return on equity exceeds the cost of equity, the price will exceed
the book value of equity; if the return on equity is lower than the cost of equity, the price will be
lower than the book value of equity.
