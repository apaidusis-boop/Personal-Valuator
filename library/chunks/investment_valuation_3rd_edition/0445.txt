We will stick with the simpler approach for three reasons. First, we are not aiming for precision in the cost of debt, but an
approximation. Given that the more complex approaches also give approximations, we will tilt in favor of transparency.
Second, there is significant correlation not only between the interest coverage ratio and bond ratings but also between the
interest coverage ratio and other ratios used in analysis, such as the debt coverage ratio and the funds flow ratios. In other
words, we may be adding little by adding other ratios that are correlated with interest coverage ratios, including
EBITDA/fixed charges, to the mix. Third, the interest coverage ratio changes as a firm changes its financing mix and
decreases as the debt ratio increases, a key requirement since we need the cost of debt to change as the debt ratio changes.
