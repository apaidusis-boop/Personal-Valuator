If the relationship is strong and linear, we could use this regression to obtain predicted price-to-book
ratios for all of the firms in the sector, separating out those firms that are undervalued from those that
are overvalued.
This regression can be enriched in two ways. The first is to allow for nonlinear relationships
between price-to-book and return on equity; this can be done either by transforming the variables
(natural logs, exponentials, etc.) or by running nonlinear regressions. The second is to expand the
regression to include other independent variables such as risk and growth.
