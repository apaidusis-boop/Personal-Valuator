IMPORTANCE OF LIMITED LIABILITY
The argument that equity is a call option holds only if equity has limited liability—that is, the most that an equity investor can
lose is what he or she has invested in a firm. This is clearly the case in publicly traded companies. In private companies,
however, the owners often have unlimited liability. If these firms get into financial trouble and are unable to make their debt
payments, the owner’s personal assets can be put at risk. You should not value equity as a call option in these cases.
