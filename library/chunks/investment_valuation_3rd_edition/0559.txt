ILLUSTRATION 19.3: Estimating the PBV Ratio for a High
Growth Firm in the Two-Stage Model
Assume that you have been asked to estimate the PBV ratio for a firm that is expected to be in high growth for the next five
years. The firm has the following characteristics:
EPS growth rate in first five years = 20%
Payout ratio in first five years = 20%
EPS growth rate after five years = 8%
Payout ratio after five years = 68%
Beta = 1.0
Risk-free rate = T-bond rate = 6%
Return on equity = 25%
Cost of equity = 6% + 1(5.5%) = 11.5% (in perpetuity)
The estimated PBV ratio for this firm is 7.89.
