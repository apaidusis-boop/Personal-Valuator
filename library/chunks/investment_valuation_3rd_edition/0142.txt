Filter Rules
In a filter rule, an investor buys an investment if the price rises X percent from a previous low and
holds the investment until the price drops X percent from a previous high. The magnitude of the
change (X percent) that triggers the trades can vary from filter rule to filter rule, with smaller changes
resulting in more transactions per period and higher transaction costs.
