3.

Value enhancement has to be firm-specific.
 No two firms in trouble share the same problems,
and using a cookbook approach seldom works in value enhancement. You have to begin by
diagnosing the specific problems faced by the firm you are analyzing and tailor a response to these
problems. Thus, the value enhancement plan you would devise for a mature firm with cost
overruns will be very different from the plan you would devise for a young firm that has a product
that no longer meets market needs.
