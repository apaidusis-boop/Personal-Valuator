From Black-Scholes to Binomial

It is a fairly simple exercise to convert the inputs to the Black-Scholes model into a binomial model.
To make the adjustment, you have to assume a multiplicative binomial process, where the magnitude
of the jumps, in percent terms, remains unchanged from period to period. If you assume symmetric
probabilities, the up (u) and down (d) movements can be estimated as a function of the annualized
variance in the price process and how many periods you decide to break each year into (t).
