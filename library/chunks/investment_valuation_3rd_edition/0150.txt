The management of a firm has some discretion on the timing of earnings reports, and there is some
evidence that the timing affects expected returns. A 1989 study of earnings reports, classified by the
day of the week that the earnings are reported, reveals that earnings and dividend reports on Fridays
are much more likely to contain negative information than announcements on any other day of the
week. This is shown in
