Three-Stage Dividend Discount Model
The three-stage dividend discount model combines the features of the two-stage model and the H
model. It allows for an initial period of high growth, a transitional period where growth declines, and
a final stable growth phase. It is the most general of the models because it does not impose any
restrictions on the payout ratio.
The Model
This model assumes an initial period of stable high growth, a second period of declining growth, and
a third period of stable low growth that lasts forever.
