## Focus on Quality

Buffett learned this lesson the hard way. Early in his career, he bought cheap companies hoping to flip them quickly—what he called "cigar butt" investing.2

The results were disappointing. Now he focuses on "wonderful companies at fair prices" rather than "fair companies at wonderful prices."3

Quality means businesses with ""—competitive advantages that protect profits over time. Coca-Cola's, American Express's extensive network, and Apple's ([AAPL](https://www.investopedia.com/markets/quote?tvwidgetsymbol=AAPL)) ecosystem all create barriers that competitors can't easily cross. These companies can raise prices, expand margins, and generate consistent returns.

Apple, which was Berkshire's biggest holding as of the end of last year, combines brand strength with recurring revenues from services—exactly the quality characteristics Buffett values.
