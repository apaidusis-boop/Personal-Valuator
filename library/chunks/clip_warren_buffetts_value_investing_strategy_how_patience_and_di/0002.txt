## Look for Undervalued Companies

Buffett searches for companies, focusing on fundamentals like low, strong cash flow, and good management.

Last year, as investors poured money into AI-related stocks, Berkshire Hathaway acquired stakes in companies many ignored, like Pool Corp. ([POOL](https://www.investopedia.com/markets/quote?tvwidgetsymbol=POOL)), which supplies swimming pool equipment, and Constellation Brands (STX), maker of Modelo beer.45

Both had stock prices that had dropped in recent years, despite still dominating their industries. Buffett perhaps sees what others miss: long-term value hiding in short-term noise.

## Think Long-Term

Buffett’s investing style is often described as boring because it involves very little trading. He once said that "our favorite holding period is forever."6

Buffett buys companies with the intention of holding them through thick and thin, as long as the business fundamentals stay intact. This allows as a strong company’s earnings and dividends can grow exponentially over decades.

Buffett’s shows that you don’t need complex formulas or rapid trades to build wealth.
