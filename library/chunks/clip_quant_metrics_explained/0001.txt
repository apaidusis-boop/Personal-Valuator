### Safety

How well a company mitigates risks to its business. We track a variety of solvency, cash flow volatility, and market measures of risk. We want to avoid anything unnecessarily risky.

- Net Debt / EBITDA -- Lower is better
- Earnings Volatility -- Lower is better
- Leverage -- Lower is better

### Valuation

Measures of the richness or cheapness of a firm as it relates to different fundamental business metrics, pursuing an optimal mix of quality and value. We don't want to pay astronomical prices for future growth.

- Gross Profits / Enterprise Value
- Cash Flow / Enterprise Value
- Adjusted Book Value / Price

### Market Buzz

While harder to describe, this boils down to a company's current or growing presence in the market zeitgeist. Does it transcend an industry? Has it become a verb? Indications of powerful adoption trends can present themselves before they show up in company financial results. When the market is excited about a company, the business gains a better chance of realizing its true value.

- Momentum
- Liquidity Trend
- Liquidity Stability

## Investing Types, Explained

As a Motley Fool member, you have access to hundreds of active recommendations (with more being added each month). That's a lot of companies to consider when building out a well-diversified portfolio of at least 50 stocks that you're comfortable holding for five or more years.

It's not always easy to decide which stocks fit your investing needs. So we're here to help!

**Investing Types** are designed to help you navigate the options, along with the rest of our Quant projections.

We've identified three core Investing Types that correspond with risk tolerance and potential returns: **Cautious**, **Moderate**, and **Aggressive**.
