### 2\. The Neutron Program — Execution Risk

Neutron is both the biggest upside catalyst and the biggest single risk. The program has been delayed repeatedly — from an initial 2024 target to late 2025, and now to mid-2026 at the earliest. A test failure in early 2026 triggered a notable stock selloff. Further delays, engine issues, or integration problems could force another timeline revision and trigger a significant re-rating. In an extreme scenario, a failed Neutron program would threaten the company's long-term trajectory.

### 3\. Stretched Valuation

RKLB trades at an exceptionally high revenue multiple, north of 50x trailing twelve-month sales as of early 2026. The stock trades at approximately 29.5x book value, versus roughly 4x for the broader US aerospace and defense sector.

This kind of premium leaves virtually no margin for error: any missed milestone, earnings shortfall, or macro headwind could produce an outsized move to the downside. The stock has already fallen more than 40% from its January 2026 peak.

### 4\. Competition

SpaceX's dominance in launch is entrenched and expanding. On the horizon, emerging rivals — including United Launch Alliance and international competitors — are investing in similar capabilities. Rocket Lab's Electron faces competition in the small-satellite launch market, while Neutron, once operational, will face Falcon 9 head-on. Technological change in the sector moves rapidly and could render current offerings less competitive.
