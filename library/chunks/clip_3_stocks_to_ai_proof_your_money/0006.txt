### The AI Tailwind Nobody Talked About

Here is the central irony that defines FIX's investment thesis: the more powerful AI becomes, the more Comfort Systems benefits. Every large language model, every AI inference cluster, every hyperscale data center requires massive amounts of cooling infrastructure, high-density power systems, and precision climate control. These are not optional upgrades. They are physical prerequisites. Without them, the servers overheat and fail.

AI systems generate extraordinary amounts of heat. The shift toward high-density GPU compute has made thermal management one of the most demanding engineering challenges in modern construction. Comfort Systems is one of a small number of companies with the workforce, technical expertise, and modular fabrication capability to deliver these systems at scale — and its $9.4 billion backlog as of Q3 2025 reflects the depth of that demand. Backlog has grown roughly 34% over two years, with technology and manufacturing clients driving the bulk of new awards.

In its Q4 2025 earnings report, the company posted EPS of $9.37 against analyst expectations of $6.77 — a beat of nearly 40%. The electrical segment, which handles data center power infrastructure, has been particularly explosive, with revenue climbing 71% year over year at peak.
