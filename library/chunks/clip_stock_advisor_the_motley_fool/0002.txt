## SPOTIFY (NYSE:SPOT)

There's a joke that Spotify's relationship with musicians is complicated.

It's not wrong. But investors who've been watching the business underneath the controversy have noticed something the critics keep missing: Spotify has quietly built the most sophisticated AI personalization engine in consumer media — and it's turning that engine into a moat.

Consider the numbers first. Spotify now has 751 million users across 184 markets, including 290 million paying subscribers. Spotify paid $11 billion to content creators in 2025 alone, and still had plenty left to reinvest in the product.

What are they investing in? AI. Specifically, AI-driven personalization at a scale no competitor can match.

At SXSW in March 2026, Co-CEO Gustav Söderström unveiled Taste Profile — a new feature that allows users to see exactly how Spotify's algorithms understand their listening behavior and actively shape it in real time. This builds on Prompted Playlists, which launched in late 2025 and allows users to describe what they want to hear in natural language, drawing on their *entire* listening history dating back to day one on the platform. Think of it as writing your own algorithm.

"If iDJ is the chat interface to Spotify," said Co-CEO Alex Norström, "Prompted Playlists is the Deep Research mode of Spotify."
