## TAKE-TWO INTERACTIVE SOFTWARE (NASDAQ:TTWO)

Most people think of Take-Two as a video game company.

They're not wrong. But that framing undersells what's actually happening inside this organization. Because the next 18 months could represent a genuine opportunity for investors.

Here's the first thing you need to understand: Take-Two is in the business of building worlds. Not games. Worlds. And with AI now embedded across its development pipeline, those worlds are becoming richer, more responsive, and more profitable than anything we've ever seen in entertainment history.

CEO Strauss Zelnick didn't mince words on the company's most recent earnings call. "We have hundreds of AI projects running now," he told investors. Not one. Not a pilot program. Hundreds. That's the scale of investment happening behind the scenes at one of the world's largest interactive entertainment companies.

And the numbers are starting to reflect it. In the third quarter of fiscal 2026, Take-Two reported net bookings of $1.76 billion, a 28% jump year-over-year that beat the company's own guidance. Management promptly raised its full-year outlook to a range of $6.65 to $6.7 billion. This is a business firing on all cylinders before its biggest catalyst has even arrived.

That catalyst, of course, is Grand Theft Auto VI.

Scheduled for release on November 19, 2026, GTA VI is arguably the most anticipated entertainment release in history. No film, no album, no streaming premiere has ever generated this level of pre-release economic attention. Analysts at Raymond James, who recently upgraded TTWO to Strong Buy, believe the title's launch will establish a new financial baseline for the business entirely. Management has guided for *record* net bookings in fiscal year 2027 — the year the game drops.

But here's what most investors miss: the real money in Take-Two's model isn't the box office moment when GTA VI launches. It's the years of recurring revenue that follow it.
